What are these metrics?

If you're not a scientist, don't worry! Here is a simple guide to what each metric means:

PM2.5 (Fine Dust): Tiny particles from car exhaust or smoke that can enter your bloodstream.
PM10 (Coarse Dust): Larger dust from construction or dirt roads that irritates the lungs.
NO2 (Nitrogen Dioxide): Toxic gas from burning fuel, causes asthma and coughing.
CO (Carbon Monoxide): Poisonous gas from vehicles and machinery.
SO2 (Sulfur Dioxide): Harsh fumes mostly from industrial factories.
O3 (Ozone): "Bad" ozone created when sunlight reacts with smog at ground level.

Health Cautionary & Advisory

Understand how different levels of AQI impact your health and what actions to take.

0 - 50: Good

Air quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.

51 - 100: Moderate

Air quality is acceptable. Unusually sensitive people should consider limiting prolonged outdoor exertion.

101 - 200: Unhealthy

Everyone may begin to experience health effects. Wear masks and avoid prolonged outdoor activity.

> 300: Hazardous

Health warning of emergency conditions. The entire population is more likely to be affected. Stay indoors, run air purifiers.

Pollutant Glossary: What Are We Measuring?

PM2.5 (Fine Particulate Matter)

Microscopic particles ( <2.5 µm) from vehicle exhaust and power plants. Can penetrate deep into the lungs and blood, causing severe respiratory/cardiovascular issues.

PM10 (Coarse Particulate Matter)

Inhalable dust particles commonly from construction sites, unpaved roads, and industrial crushing operations. Triggers asthma attacks.

NO2 (Nitrogen Dioxide)

A highly reactive, suffocating gas primarily emitted by heavy vehicle traffic and fossil fuel power stations.

SO2 (Sulfur Dioxide)

A toxic gas produced by burning sulfur-heavy fossil fuels like coal and crude oil. The main catalyst for acid rain.

CO (Carbon Monoxide)

A colorless, odorless, deadly gas resulting from the incomplete burning of carbon fuels, heavily tied to dense traffic jams.

O3 (Ground-Level Ozone)

A reactive gas created when sunlight bakes other pollutants (like NO2). It forms the main chemical ingredient in visible smog.
